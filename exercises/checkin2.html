Pigeon Facts

Columbidae family

Columbidae is a bird family consisting of doves and pigeons. Smaller species tend to be called “doves” and larger ones “pigeons”, although there is no scientific distinction between the two terms. The Columbidae family is divided into 353 species. Pigeons and doves are distributed everywhere on earth, except for the driest areas of the Sahara Desert, Antarctica, and the high Arctic. The largest geographic range of an Columbidae species is that of the rock dove, also called the common pigeon.

Most Common Columbidae Species by Population

  1. Mourning dove: 475 million
  2. Rock dove: 260 million
  3. Eurasian collared dove: 20 - 50 million
  4. European turtle dove: 12.8 - 47.6 million
  5. Pale-vented pigeon: 5 - 50 million

Passenger pigeons

  • Extinct species of Columbidae
  • Vast numbers lived in North America for tens of thousands of years
  • Traveled in huge flocks that were said to blacken the sky
  • Extinction driven by commercial hunting and habitat loss caused by European colonists

Martha

The last known passenger pigeon was a bird named Martha, named after Martha Washington, who died in 1914 at the Cincinnati Zoo.
